About

I am a researcher working at the intersection of computer vision, deep learning, and atmospheric science. My work focuses on developing neural network architectures that can predict air pollution at unprecedented spatial resolutions.

My current research introduces TopoFlow, a multi-scale vision transformer that combines global meteorological context (ERA5, CAMS) with local high-resolution observations (GHAP, elevation) through cross-attention mechanisms. The model produces daily PM2.5 forecasts at 0.01° (~1 km) resolution across Europe, outperforming operational systems like CAMS and foundation models like Aurora.

I train and deploy models on the LUMI supercomputer (Europe's fastest), leveraging multi-node distributed training on AMD MI250X GPUs with PyTorch DDP.

Research Projects

Featured

TopoFlow Europe

Multi-Scale Vision Transformer for High-Resolution PM2.5 Forecasting

A dual-branch architecture combining a global ViT (ERA5 + CAMS at 0.25°) with a local ViT (GHAP + elevation at 0.01°) through cross-attention. Delta prediction formulation enables learning corrections from persistence baseline. Evaluated on 362 days over Europe (2022), achieving RMSE = 5.99 and r = 0.74, outperforming CAMS (-47% RMSE) and persistence baselines.

Wind-Aware Topological Air Quality Prediction
