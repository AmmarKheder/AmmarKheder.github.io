About

I am a researcher working at the intersection of computer vision, deep learning, and atmospheric science. I develop neural network architectures for air pollution prediction at unprecedented spatial resolutions.

My work includes TopoFlow, a multi-scale vision transformer producing daily PM2.5 forecasts at 0.01° (~1 km) across Europe, and AQ-Net, a deep learning framework for regional air quality estimation. I train and deploy models on the LUMI supercomputer using multi-node distributed training on AMD MI250X GPUs.
